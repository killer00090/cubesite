orientation of the white cross.


First thing we should do before you read this is to

1. Understand the Cube's Structure

  • Faces

  • The Rubik's Cube has 6 faces (Front, Back, Left, Right, Up, Down), e ach face is a different colour. The colours should be this pattern, red is opposite to orange, blue is opposite to green, green is next to orang e and red, blue is next is next to red and orange, white is opposite to yellow, and white and yellow is next to all colours.

  • Center Pieces

  • The center pieces determine the color of each face and do not move r elative to each other. This means that the face pieces will never change it's opposite until you remove the piece entirely.

  • Edge Pieces

  • These pieces have two colors and are located between the corners. Th ey are always touching the center pieces of the cube.

  • Corner Pieces

  • These have three colors and are located at the corners of the cube. They are always touching the edge pieces, and for speedcubes, never touch the edge. (A speedcube is a Rubik's Cube built to turn really fast and are either really expensive, or really cheap.)
Now onto the first step.

Creating the Daisy

Objective: Form a cross on the yellow face with the edg e pieces matching the center colors of adjacent faces.

Step 1: Locate the White Center Piece

  • Find the yellow center piece on one face. This will be your reference po int for the white side, and the white cross' center.
  • Find the White Edge Pieces: Edge pieces are those with white on them but also have another color (e.g., white-red, white-blue).
  • Position the White Edge Pieces:
    • Step 2: Rotate the top layer (the face with the yellow center piece) until one of the white edge pieces is directly above its correct colour on the middle layer. For example, if you see a white edge piece with red , it should be above the red center piece.
    • Step 3: Rotate the layer with the center pieces you just aligned to the white center piece.
    • Repeat step 2 and 3 to the other unoriented pieces

The goal is to form a white cross on the top face of th e cube.


The white edge pieces should be correctly positioned, aligning with the cent er pieces of the adjacent faces. For example, the white-red edge should be betwe en the white center piece and the red center piece.
Make sure to check the alignment of each edge piece. If even one edge piece is wrong, then not only will you look very dumb, but the cube won't be solved pr operly. (Other steps may look different compared to how they are supposed to if you mess up.)

Step 2: Solve the White Cross Correctly

Objective: Make sure that not only is the white cross c omplete, but each edge piece is aligned with the correct center piece on the adj acent faces.

Align the White Edges.

Again, if a white edge piece is not correctly aligned with the center piece of t he adjacent face, you'll need to adjust it.
Example: If the white-blue edge piece is on the white face but not matching the blue center piece,

do the following:

  • Move the edge piece to the correct position on the top layer.
  • Rotate the top layer to align it with the center piece.

    • Correct Misalignments

    • If an edge piece is in the correct position but not oriented correctly, use the following sequence:
    • Move the white edge piece to the top face.
    • Rotate the face with the edge piece to align it with the center piece.
    • Perform a move to get the edge piece out of the top layer, then re-align and place it back.
If you did this right, you should have a white cross that has the colours match the centers, has no edges that are flipped, and all edges are in the right spot.
